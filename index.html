About Me

I'm a Physics MSc student enrolled in the University of Coimbra. I'm pationate about understanding the fundamental laws that govern our universe and the models that we can devise to describe it. I find it fascinating how it is possible for us to create mathematical and physical models that represent a variety of systems at disctinct scales and with different levels of abstraction. Within my area of study, I found a special interest in quantum, condensed matter and statistical physics as well as optics. My goal is to expand my knowledge on these topics and learn mathematical tools that will allow me to study a variety of systems, in particular, biological ones.

As a hobby, I enjoy solving programming puzzles while learning and implementing algorithms. In the future, I hope to marry my interests in programming, biology and physics to create and implement mathematical models of biological systems.

Research and Work Experience

IAstro’s Astronomy and Science Communication Summer Internship (July 2021 - June 2022)

Instituto de Astrofísica e Ciências do Espaço (IA)
Supervisors: Nuno Santos and João Gomes da Silva

https://divulgacao.iastro.pt/en/iastro-summer-internships-2023
  • During this internship I attended several science communication workshops and worked on a research project.
  • My project was titled "Finding the best way to model stellar activity noise with the hydrogen line"

On this two week project, my partner and I worked on the analysis of stellar activity of low temperature stars. We obtained results that are new to the literature and may help researchers in the future to better rely on the Radial Velocity Method as a diagnosis tool for the detection of exoplanets orbiting FGK stars.

After the internship I continued to work with my partner and advisers to expand the study to a larger data set and the findings have been published in Astronomy and Astrophysics.
